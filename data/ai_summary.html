AI Nutrition Summary 2023 – 2025 · Delhaize · 2 500 kcal/day reference
✓ Strengths
  • Very low added sugar (1–9 % DRV). Minimal processed sweets and no regular soda purchases — exceptional for a modern Western diet.
  • Vitamin A improving sharply (79 % → 182 % DRV). Driven by consistent mango, carrot and dairy purchases. Excellent trend.
  • B12 and Zinc well covered (~150–250 % DRV). Heavy meat and dairy basket guarantees these stay adequate year-round.
  • Fiber nearly on target in 2025 (96 % DRV), up from 60 % in 2023. The increase in fruit, legumes and whole-grain bread is paying off.
  • Good fruit variety: avocado (67×), mango (59×), apple (52×), banana (52×), kiwi (17×), clementine (21×). Strong Vitamin C sources.
⚠ Concerns
  • Vitamin D chronically low (21–28 % DRV). Belgium receives limited sun, and the 15 salmon purchases per year are not enough. This is the single most actionable gap — a daily supplement of 1 000–2 000 IU is recommended for the whole household, particularly October–April.
  • Magnesium below target (49–75 % DRV, improving). The basket is dominated by foods poor in magnesium: bread, pork, beef, mozzarella, ham. Add more nuts (almonds, cashews), legumes (beans, lentils beyond hummus), dark leafy greens and whole grains.
  • Sodium 3× the reference (293–303 % DRV) every year. The main drivers are cured/processed meats (ham 67×, pork 106×, salami, lardons) and bread. Look for lower-salt ham and bread alternatives; cook from scratch where possible to avoid hidden salt in processed items.
  • Saturated fat 245–309 % DRV. Three years of consistently high saturated fat intake from butter, creme fraîche (52×), mozzarella (74×), pork and red meat. Consider replacing some butter with olive oil, swapping full-fat creme fraîche for lighter alternatives, and limiting red meat to 3–4 portions per week.
  • Protein at 63–72 % DRV against a 160 g reference. The actual intake of 100–115 g/day is adequate for most non-athletic adults; the gap reflects the high reference value used (EFSA population standard). No action needed unless household includes athletes or children with high needs.
↑ Trend 2023 → 2025
Positive trajectory across nearly all micronutrients over three years: Fiber +35 pp, Vitamin A +103 pp, B12 +89 pp, Calcium +22 pp, Magnesium +26 pp, Vitamin D +7 pp. The two persistent problems — sodium and saturated fat — have barely moved and deserve focused attention in the coming year.
